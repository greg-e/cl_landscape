Emailing Invoices from QuickBooks Desktop

Admin Reference Guide  ·  QuickBooks Desktop 2019

CL Landscape
Prepared April 2026
QuickBooks is configured to send invoices directly through the Outlook desktop app using the jose.ramos@cl-land.com mailbox. The steps below cover daily use — sending one invoice at a time and batching multiple invoices — plus quick fixes for common issues.

1   Send a Single Invoice

  1. In QuickBooks, open the invoice: Customers → Create Invoices, then find or create the invoice.
  2. Verify the customer's email address is shown in the Bill To area. If it's missing, update the customer record first (see Section 4).
  3. Make sure the "To be e-mailed" checkbox at the top of the invoice is checked.
  4. Click the envelope icon (Email) at the top of the invoice window, or go to File → Send Forms.
  5. A preview window opens showing the recipient address and subject line. Review it, then click Send Now.
  6. Outlook will briefly open in the background and send the email. The invoice status will update to Sent.
Tip: After sending, open Outlook and check Sent Items to confirm the email went out with the PDF attached.

2   Send a Batch of Invoices

  1. Go to File → Send Forms.
  2. QuickBooks lists every invoice that has "To be e-mailed" checked.
  3. Review the list. Uncheck any invoices you do not want to send right now.
  4. Click Send Now. QuickBooks will email each invoice one at a time through Outlook.
  5. When finished, open Outlook → Sent Items and spot-check 2–3 emails to confirm delivery.
Note: Outlook must be open (but not actively composing a new email) while QuickBooks is sending the batch.

3   Verify Delivery

In Outlook

  1. Open Outlook and go to Sent Items.
  2. Find the invoice email — confirm the PDF is attached.
  3. If the email is missing, the send failed (see Section 5).

In QuickBooks

  1. Open the invoice that was sent.
  2. Look for the "Sent" label or email icon status near the top.
  3. The history tab will also log the send date and recipient.

4   Add or Update a Customer's Email Address

  1. Go to Customers → Customer Center.
  2. Double-click the customer's name to open their record.
  3. Click the Address Info tab.
  4. Enter or correct the email address in the Main Email field.
  5. Click OK to save. The next invoice created for this customer will include the email address automatically.

5   Quick Troubleshooting

Problem What to do
Invoice not in Outlook Sent Items Make sure Outlook is open before sending. Try again via File → Send Forms. If it still fails, restart both Outlook and QuickBooks, then retry.
QuickBooks freezes or crashes on send Update QuickBooks: Help → Update QuickBooks Desktop. Also check that Outlook is open and not composing another email at the time of the send.
Customer says invoice went to spam Ask the customer to mark the email as "Not Spam" and add jose.ramos@cl-land.com to their contacts. This is a one-time fix per customer.
Customer says no PDF was attached In QuickBooks, go to Help → QuickBooks PDF & Print Repair Tool and run the repair. Then resend the invoice.
"To be e-mailed" checkbox is missing The customer record has no email address. Add it in Customer Center (see Section 4), then reopen the invoice — the checkbox will appear.
Outlook does not open at all when sending Outlook may not be set as the default mail app. Contact your IT support to verify the Windows default app setting and QuickBooks Send Forms preference.

6   Daily Send Checklist

  • Outlook is open before starting
  • Customer has a valid email address on file
  • "To be e-mailed" box is checked on each invoice
  • Reviewed invoice amounts and due dates before sending
  • Clicked Send Now and saw the confirmation
  • Checked Outlook Sent Items — email present with PDF
  • Invoice status in QuickBooks shows "Sent"
