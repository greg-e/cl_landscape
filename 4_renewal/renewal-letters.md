# Renewal Letters by Strategy

Use these templates when you send the renewal contract package.

## Contract Package Checklist (Reference)

Attach these items with each email:
- Renewal Agreement (signature copy)
- Scope of Work (old vs new scope comparison)
- Pricing Schedule (base service, add-ons, surcharges if applicable)
- Service Calendar or Frequency Summary
- Certificate of Insurance (if requested)

Quick fields to fill in before sending:
- Contract Term: [Start Date] to [End Date]
- Renewal Effective Date: [Effective Date]
- Billing Terms: [Net 15 / Net 30 / Due on Receipt]
- Late Fee Terms: [Late Fee Language]
- Notice to Cancel/Change: [30/60/90 days written notice]
- Signature Deadline: [Date]
- Primary Contact for Operations: [Name / Email / Phone]

## Optional One-Line Inserts

Use these lines when you want to explain the reasoning briefly.

- "This update reflects route distance, travel time, and seasonal needs."
- "The revised structure helps us keep service steady during busy months."
- "We kept the scope clear so you can compare the current and renewed service side by side."
- "We can also provide another frequency option if you want to keep the budget tighter."

## Optional Contract Language Inserts

Use these as needed in any letter:

- "Service will continue without interruption once we receive the signed agreement."
- "Pricing shown excludes extraordinary or out-of-scope work unless the agreement says otherwise."
- "Any requested scope changes after signature will be handled through a written change order."
- "Invoices will be issued [monthly/quarterly] according to the billing terms above."
- "Please have an authorized signer execute the agreement to avoid delays."
