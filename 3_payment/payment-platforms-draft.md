# Payment Platform Draft: "All Payment Methods" for Small Business

## Question

Are there services that handle all payment methods and make payments easy for a small business?

## Short Answer

No single platform truly handles every payment method end-to-end (especially checks and Zelle), but several platforms cover most customer preferences with low operational friction.

## Best Near-All-In-One Options

### 1) Square

What it covers:
- Online invoices and payment links
- Card payments
- In-person tap/chip
- Mobile wallets (Apple Pay and Google Pay)
- ACH support in many cases

Pros:
- Very easy setup for small businesses
- Strong mix of in-person and remote payments
- Good user experience for customers

Cons:
- Does not replace check processing workflow
- Zelle remains separate
- Processing fees apply on card rails

Best fit:
- Businesses that want fast deployment and one platform for both field and online payments

### 2) Stripe

What it covers:
- Hosted payment links and online checkout
- Card payments
- Mobile wallets
- Bank transfer options including ACH (configuration dependent)

Pros:
- Excellent checkout experience
- Highly flexible and scalable
- Strong payment tooling

Cons:
- More setup/admin complexity than Square
- Checks and Zelle remain outside Stripe
- Processing fees apply

Best fit:
- Businesses prioritizing polished online payment experience and flexibility

### 3) QuickBooks Payments

What it covers:
- QuickBooks-integrated invoice payments
- Cards and ACH (plan/region dependent)

Pros:
- Simplifies accounting reconciliation when integrated
- Easy invoice-to-payment workflow
- Good fit for teams already centered on QuickBooks

Cons:
- Compatibility and feature depth depend on QuickBooks version/setup
- Not a universal channel for all payment types
- Checks and Zelle remain separate workflows

Best fit:
- Businesses optimizing for accounting simplicity first

### 4) PayPal (plus Venmo in supported flows)

What it covers:
- PayPal checkout and invoicing
- Card acceptance through PayPal
- Venmo options in some configurations

Pros:
- Very familiar to many consumers
- Fast customer adoption in residential segments
- Easy to share payment links

Cons:
- Dispute/hold management can be more involved
- Not a complete replacement for check and bank-direct workflows
- Fees apply

Best fit:
- Customer bases already comfortable with PayPal and Venmo

## Practical Reality for Small Business

"All methods" is usually a hybrid stack:

1. Primary platform (Square, Stripe, or QuickBooks Payments)
2. Check acceptance process
3. Optional Zelle bridge while other methods mature

This is typically the most practical way to maximize customer choice without creating operational confusion.

## Suggested Decision Criteria

Compare options on:
- Customer convenience
- Ease of setup
- Accounting reconciliation effort
- Total processing cost
- Support for in-person and remote payments

## Draft Recommendation Pattern

If the goal is customer ease with manageable operations:

1. Pick one primary platform (Square, Stripe, or QuickBooks Payments)
2. Keep checks available
3. Keep Zelle temporarily if customers already use it
4. Reassess after 30 days using actual usage and days-to-pay data
